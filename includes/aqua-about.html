Aqua

I am Aqua , latin for water . I was launched on May 4, 2002 as part of the Earth observing system ( EOS) for NASA in the Afternoon constellation. My mission is to collect data on Earth's water cycle , including evaporation from oceans , water vapor in the atmosphere , clouds , precipitation , soil moisture and Ice from seas, land ,snow .

.Thanks to my afternoon equatorial crossing time I was formerly named EOS PM . My timeline on-orbit progress through the initial 120 day check-out period can be found here.

Nasa eqiupped me with six Earth-observing instruments to help me with my mission to collect a variety of global data sets. While I was originally developed for a six-year design life, I have now far exceeded that original goal. I continue transmitting high-quality data from four of my six instruments, AIRS, AMSU, CERES, and MODIS, and reduced quality data from a fifth instrument, AMSR-E. The sixth instrument, HSB, collected approximately nine months of high quality data but failed in February 2003.

I was the first member launched of a group of satellites termed the Afternoon Constellation, or sometimes the A-Train. The second member to be launched was Aura, in July 2004, the third member was PARASOL, in December 2004, and the fourth and fifth members are CloudSat and CALIPSO, in May 2006, and the sixth member was GCOM-W1 in May 2012. In December 2013, PARASOL completed its mission and exited the A-Train. The latest addition to the A-Train was OCO-2, launched in July 2014. Now complete, the A-Train is led by OCO-2, followed by GCOM-W1, then Aqua, CALIPSO, CloudSat and, in the rear, Aura.
